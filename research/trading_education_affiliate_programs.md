## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

After researching 40+ trading education, course, mentorship, signals, and book affiliate programs, I identified **18 programs with confirmed, actionable affiliate details** and **12 programs where details could not be publicly verified**. The realistic near-term revenue opportunities for a trading tools review/directory site like TradingToolsHub are concentrated in a handful of high-quality programs that (a) accept content/review sites, (b) pay meaningful commissions, and (c) have products relevant to TTH's audience.

**Top Priority Programs for TTH (realistic to join, good fit):**
1. Benzinga Pro -- 30% recurring, 90-day cookie, via Impact
2. Motley Fool -- $100-$700 CPA, 30-45 day cookie, via Impact/Awin
3. Seeking Alpha -- $90 CPA, 30-day cookie, direct
4. Investors Underground -- 20% commission, 60-day cookie, direct
5. StocksToTrade -- 20% recurring, direct
6. Topstep -- 15-25% recurring, 30-day cookie, direct
7. Capitalist Exploits (Insider) -- 25% lifetime recurring ($625/yr/customer), direct
8. Market Rebellion -- 100% first month, via Awin
9. Zacks -- $70 CPA, 30-day cookie, via CJ
10. Udemy -- 10-15%, 7-day cookie, via Rakuten
11. Amazon Associates -- 4.5% on books, 24-hr cookie, direct

**Honest Assessment:** Most trading education companies (Warrior Trading, Bear Bull Traders, Humbled Trader, SMB Capital, ClayTrader, The Trading Channel, ZipTrader, Ricky Gutierrez/Techbuds, Sang Lucci) either do not have public affiliate programs, have invite-only/student-only programs, or do not publish their terms. This is a significant limitation. The "influencer education" space tends to rely on YouTube/social referrals rather than traditional affiliate partnerships with review sites.

- TTH needs significantly more traffic before affiliate revenue becomes meaningful

**Near-term (0-6 months, current traffic):**

- Most value from: informational content that ranks, building trust
- Action: Sign up for programs, add links to existing reviews, but don't expect revenue

## KEY OBSERVATIONS

1. **The trading education affiliate space is less mature than trading tools/brokers.** Most education companies rely on YouTube/social influencers rather than traditional affiliate networks. This means fewer structured programs with published terms.

2. **The best opportunities are in research/analysis tools, not pure education.** Benzinga Pro, Seeking Alpha, Zacks, and Motley Fool have well-structured affiliate programs because they are subscription services, not personality-driven courses.

3. **High-ticket education programs (Warrior Trading, SMB Capital, Simpler Trading) would pay the most per sale but are hardest to access.** These programs typically only work with established influencers or current students.

4. **Recurring commissions beat CPA for long-term revenue.** Benzinga (30% recurring) and Topstep (15-25% recurring) will compound over time, while Motley Fool ($100 CPA) is a one-time payment.

5. **TTH's current traffic is too low for meaningful affiliate revenue.** The priority should be growing organic traffic through quality content, with affiliate links embedded for when traffic arrives. Signing up now ensures links are in place when traffic scales.

6. **Amazon Associates is low-margin but high-trust.** Trading book recommendations convert well and add credibility, even at 4.5% commission.
