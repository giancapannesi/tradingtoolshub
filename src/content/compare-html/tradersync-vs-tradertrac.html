Short answer: choose TraderSync if you want a broader paid journal with mobile workflow, market replay and a more established import-heavy product. Choose TraderTrac if you want a lighter AI-first journal with lower cost, fast capture and paid IBKR auto-import.

Where TraderSync Wins

TraderSync is stronger for traders who want a bigger paid journaling platform. Its positioning is around imports, analytics, mobile access, replay and AI-assisted review. That makes it attractive for active traders who already know they will use a journal every week and want more product depth than a lightweight workflow.

The mobile angle also matters. TraderTrac is web-based and mobile responsive, but it does not currently have a native mobile app listed as a strength. If mobile review is central to the way you work, TraderSync deserves serious consideration.

Where TraderTrac Wins

TraderTrac wins when simplicity and price are the deciding factors. The free tier lets a trader start logging without committing to another subscription, and Pro is cheaper than TraderSync's paid plans. It also has a clear AI psychology-review angle, which fits traders who want the journal to call out behavior patterns rather than only organize trade records.

For IBKR users, TraderTrac's paid SnapTrade import is an important point. It means the product is not only manual logging anymore, while still keeping manual capture available for trade thesis, emotion and execution notes.
