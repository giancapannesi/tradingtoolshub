Verdict: thinkorswim is the deeper platform for serious options traders — 25 years of options-native DNA, thinkScript backtesting, and a full six-asset-class reach. Power E*TRADE is the cleaner, faster on-ramp for E*TRADE customers who want strong options tools without the complexity, but you lose scripting, backtesting, and non-US markets.

What each platform actually costs

Both platforms are free — there is no monthly subscription and no platform fee on either side. What you pay is the per-contract commission on the parent broker: $0.65 per options contract at Schwab (thinkorswim) and $0.65 at E*TRADE, dropping to $0.50 per contract for active traders on Power E*TRADE. Stock and ETF trades are $0 commission on both. Futures cost $2.25 per side per contract on thinkorswim; Power E*TRADE routes to E*TRADE's futures desk with comparable pricing. The real cost delta is on non-US instruments: thinkorswim covers 65+ forex pairs and spot plus futures crypto, while Power E*TRADE is US stocks, options, and futures only.

Options analytics depth

This is where thinkorswim's 1999 options-trading DNA shows. Its options chain sits directly inside the chart, with real-time Greeks, probability-of-touch and probability-in-the-money overlays, risk-profile visualisations for any spread, and multi-leg construction across every standard strategy. Power E*TRADE counters with Strategy Optimizer (suggests structures based on your directional view), Options Analyzer for P&L modelling, Snapshot Analysis for at-a-glance risk/reward, and up-to-four-leg orders in a single ticket. For a trader running iron condors, butterflies, or diagonals daily, thinkorswim's depth wins; for a trader who wants opinionated suggestions without building the mental model themselves, Power E*TRADE's Strategy Optimizer is a genuine differentiator.

Scripting, backtesting, and automation

thinkorswim has thinkScript — a full scripting language for custom indicators, scanners, and strategy backtests. Power E*TRADE has none of this. It has no backtesting, no custom indicator language, and no automated trading. If you plan to encode any of your options edges into a repeatable, testable rule set, thinkorswim is the only choice between these two. thinkorswim also exposes the (limited, retail-inconsistent) Schwab Trader API; Power E*TRADE offers no API path at all.

Charting and platform breadth

thinkorswim ships approximately 450 built-in technical studies and rivals dedicated charting products; Power E*TRADE has more than 100 studies with customisable drawing tools and integrated Reuters news. On markets covered, thinkorswim spans stocks, options, futures, forex, crypto, and ETFs in one interface, versus Power E*TRADE's stocks-options-futures scope. If your options trading regularly touches futures options, thinkorswim consolidates the workflow.

Learning curve reality

thinkorswim is dense by design — the desktop UI packs institutional-grade tools behind menus that reward hours of exploration. Power E*TRADE's web platform is materially easier to onboard and its mobile app is more consistent with the desktop experience. New options traders often start on Power E*TRADE and graduate to thinkorswim after their strategies get more complex. Both offer full paper-trading environments (paperMoney and Paper Trading by Power E*TRADE) with live market data, so testing before committing is free on either side.

Broker-parent trade-offs

Choosing the platform means choosing the broker. thinkorswim requires a Charles Schwab account (Schwab absorbed TD Ameritrade in the 2020 acquisition, with full platform migration completed May 2024). Power E*TRADE requires an E*TRADE account, which is now a Morgan Stanley subsidiary. Both are large, well-capitalised US brokers with SIPC protection; the practical differences are margin rates, cash-sweep yields, and research access (Morgan Stanley research is embedded in Power E*TRADE; Schwab research is embedded in thinkorswim). Neither platform is portable to a competing broker.

Which should you choose?

  • Choose thinkorswim if you trade multi-leg options daily, want to backtest strategies with thinkScript, need futures options in the same interface, or plan to grow into API-driven work.
  • Choose Power E*TRADE if you're already an E*TRADE customer, want a cleaner learning curve, value the Strategy Optimizer's suggestive UX, or trade primarily directional options rather than complex spreads.
  • Choose neither yet if you haven't paper-traded both — the free paper-trading feature on each removes any excuse to commit real capital before you've tested the workflow.
