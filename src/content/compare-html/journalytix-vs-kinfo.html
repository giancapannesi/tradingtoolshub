Short answer: choose Journalytix if you are a futures trader who wants real-time journaling tied to the trading session. Choose Kinfo if the goal is broker-verified performance tracking, public proof of results, and a lower-cost stock/options journal.

The Real Difference

Journalytix and Kinfo are both journals, but they solve different problems. Journalytix is built around the active futures workflow: the trade happens, the journal records it, and the review includes session context such as risk, execution timing and market events. It is a better fit when missing a trade, breaking a prop-firm rule or trading through a news event is the thing that costs money.

Kinfo is closer to a verified track-record product. Its official positioning is about connecting a broker, importing transactions and proving performance with verified data. That makes it useful for traders who want to share results, audit their own long-term behavior, or avoid screenshots and manually edited spreadsheets.

Pricing and Commitment

Journalytix uses a paid specialist model. The stored TTH profile has it at $47 per month or $399 per year, and the page should be checked from the official Journalytix site before purchase because trading platforms change plans often. The important buyer point is not only price; it is whether real-time futures capture saves enough review time to justify a specialist subscription.

Kinfo currently lists a free plan, Pro at $24.90 per month and Pro+ at $44.90 per month on its official pricing page. That gives Kinfo an easier starting path and a paid ceiling that still stays close to Journalytix. If a trader only needs verified broker sync and performance reporting, Kinfo is the cheaper and simpler place to start.

Where Journalytix Wins

Journalytix wins when the journal needs to be part of the trading session itself. For active futures traders, especially those using NinjaTrader-style workflows or funded-account rules, a next-day spreadsheet is often too late. The journal has to capture behavior while the trade context still exists.

The value is clearest for traders who repeatedly ask: did I take the right setup, did I trade around news, did I exceed risk, and did I behave differently during volatile periods? Those are futures-specific questions. Kinfo can verify performance, but it is not trying to be a real-time futures coach.

Where Kinfo Wins

Kinfo wins on proof, simplicity and cost. The broker connection is the point: trades are pulled from the account, performance is calculated from actual activity, and the trader can build a track record that is harder to manipulate. That is useful for social traders, educators, signal sellers and anyone trying to separate real performance from marketing claims.

Kinfo is also easier to recommend to a trader who has not proved they will journal every week. A free plan plus paid tiers creates a smoother adoption path. Journalytix can be worth the money, but only if the trader is already serious enough to use the real-time workflow.

Bottom Line

Journalytix is the better product for active futures traders who need session-aware journaling. Kinfo is the better product for stock and options traders who care about verified performance, social proof and a lower-cost entry path.

The practical test is simple: if you would be disappointed because the journal did not capture what happened during the futures session, start with Journalytix. If you would be disappointed because nobody can verify whether your results are real, start with Kinfo. Those are different jobs, and buying the wrong one creates a journal you stop using.
