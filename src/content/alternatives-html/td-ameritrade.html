Quick verdict: TD Ameritrade alternatives now mean Schwab alternatives

TD Ameritrade is not a normal broker-shopping decision anymore. Charles Schwab's own migration page says TD Ameritrade has been acquired by Schwab, and Schwab now positions trading as powered by Ameritrade through the thinkorswim platform. That means a trader searching for TD Ameritrade alternatives is usually asking one of two questions: what replaced TD Ameritrade, or what should I use if Schwab's version of the workflow does not fit?

The honest answer is that thinkorswim remains the center of the TD Ameritrade legacy. If you loved TD Ameritrade for charting, options analytics, paperMoney and trader education, the first comparison should be Charles Schwab and thinkorswim. If you wanted TD Ameritrade as a general brokerage brand, the best alternatives are Fidelity, E*TRADE, Interactive Brokers and the mobile-first brokers depending on your actual trading style.

Where the TD Ameritrade stack still wins

The old TD Ameritrade appeal was a rare mix: a large retail brokerage plus a serious active-trading platform. Most alternatives only match one side of that. Fidelity is excellent as a household brokerage but does not feel like thinkorswim. Interactive Brokers is stronger for professional access but less friendly. E*TRADE is strong for retail options but does not replace every thinkorswim habit.

For many traders, the right answer is to stay with Schwab for the thinkorswim continuation and add specialist tools around it: TraderTrac for journaling, StockCharts for breadth and technical scans, or TradingView for charting and alerts.

What to check before choosing a replacement

  • Do not compare against the old brand only: compare against Schwab plus thinkorswim, because that is the practical successor.
  • List the platform features you actually use: paper trading, options analytics, futures access, alerts and chart studies point to different replacements.
  • Separate brokerage from tools: you may not need to move assets if the missing piece is a scanner, journal or charting app.
  • Check account migration details: old TD Ameritrade account expectations should be verified against current Schwab account and fee pages.
