Trademetria is most useful for traders and investors who need one journal to combine multiple asset classes, portfolios and cash movements. Its current product covers equities, options, futures, forex, CFDs and crypto, with portfolio tracking, broker imports, simulations, AI-assisted analysis and a permanent free plan. The main buying decision is not whether features exist—it is whether the account and order limits fit the user’s actual volume.

This review was checked against Trademetria’s official pricing, help and legal pages on July 18, 2026. Earlier TradingToolsHub pricing was stale and has been replaced below.

The free plan is limited, but it is not useless

Thirty orders per month and three open positions are too restrictive for an active day trader, especially because an order count is not necessarily the same as a completed round-trip trade. The free plan can still be useful for a low-frequency swing trader, a long-term investor, or someone testing one broker workflow before paying.

Trademetria also offers a separate full-feature trial measured by usage rather than calendar time. New users can test all features for up to 200 page views and upload up to 1,000 executions. When the page-view allowance is exhausted, the account drops to the free plan and paid features require an upgrade.

This is a better evaluation opportunity than the existing review suggested, but users should plan the test. Import a representative sample, reconcile it, inspect the reports and try the AI tools before using page views on casual navigation.

Refund restrictions make the pre-subscription test important

Trademetria does not offer a general change-of-mind refund after payment. Its current terms say refunds are issued when coding errors persist after notification and cannot be resolved. Pro-rated refunds are described for Basic-to-Pro upgrades. Canceling stops future renewal but does not create a refund for the unused part of the paid period.

That policy makes the 200-page trial the real purchase-protection mechanism. Test the exact broker, instruments, options handling, currency behavior and reports before subscribing. The details appear in Trademetria’s current terms and help center.

Multi-asset support has important boundaries

Trademetria supports equities globally and covers popular futures markets in the United States, Europe, India and Brazil. Standard equity options are supported where the contract multiplier is 100. Some illiquid options on futures are not supported. CFDs depend on broker CSV imports.

Crypto support has additional constraints: values are supported up to eight decimal places, but leveraged crypto contracts are not supported according to the current help page. Multiple crypto currencies are not converted into one base currency, so aggregated results can contain mixed currency units.

Forex manual entry also requires care because the documented quantity convention uses one unit to represent 100,000 currency units. Anyone trading unusual contracts or exchanges should confirm symbol and multiplier handling before relying on portfolio totals.

“Broker supported” does not always mean automatic sync

Trademetria says it supports more than 140 brokers, but import methods differ. Some brokers have API auto-import, while others require a broker CSV, a Trademetria template or manual entry. A platform appearing on the supported list does not prove that continuous synchronization is available.

Check the two separate columns—file upload and auto import/sync—for the exact broker. Then reconcile fills, commissions, corporate actions, options multipliers and partial executions against the broker statement. An analytics platform cannot correct an incomplete source export without intervention.

For example, Trademetria’s CQG QTrader instructions use an exported filled-orders file rather than claiming a live connection. This distinction matters to traders who expect a journal to update automatically after every session.

Portfolio tracking is the strongest reason to choose Trademetria

Many journals focus narrowly on closed day trades. Trademetria also tracks open positions, deposits, withdrawals, dividends, splits, fees, cash transactions, portfolio value and cost basis. That makes it better suited to someone combining active trading with longer-term holdings or operating several account types.

Pro supports up to 50 accounts, while Basic remains limited to one. A trader with two brokers therefore needs Pro even if total order volume is below Basic’s 500-order allowance. Buyers should map both dimensions—volume and account count—before selecting a tier.

Portfolio aggregation is only trustworthy when currencies and instrument specifications are normalized. Mixed-currency crypto results and unsupported derivatives should be kept in separate views rather than rolled into a misleading total.

AI Insights exists, but it still depends on clean data

The current plan table includes AI Insights and AI Assistant across Free, Basic and Pro. The previous review’s claim that AI analysis was absent or inherently shallow was not supported by current first-party evidence and has been removed.

AI output should be treated as a prompt for investigation, not proof of a trading edge. Missing fills, inconsistent strategy names or mixed market regimes can produce plausible but weak conclusions. Confirm every suggested pattern in the underlying report, require a meaningful sample and validate changes on unseen trades.

The P&L Simulator is useful for testing changes to exits or rules, but it carries the same overfitting risk. Separate the trades used to discover an idea from those used to evaluate it.

Cloud access is different from a dedicated mobile app

Trademetria is a cloud application, so restricting it to a desktop-access label is misleading. The journal can be accessed through a browser without installing desktop software, and broker exports may sometimes be downloaded from mobile broker apps.

A browser-accessible service is not automatically equivalent to a native iOS or Android application. Traders who need offline entry, push notifications or a particular mobile workflow should test those requirements during the trial rather than relying on a generic mobile-app label.

Data ownership and security deserve a pre-import check

Trademetria states that it does not sell or monetize trading data and funds the service through subscriptions. Its help center describes TLS-protected communication, encrypted passwords, daily backups, security testing and payment processing through Stripe or PayPal.

The help page also says data remains after a plan downgrade until the user terminates the account. Before importing sensitive history, review current privacy and deletion terms, use a unique password, and retain original broker statements outside the platform.

Who should choose Trademetria?

Choose Trademetria when multi-asset portfolio tracking, cash-flow records, long-term holdings and trading analytics need to live together. The Free plan fits light usage; Basic fits one active account below 500 monthly orders; Pro is the practical choice for multiple accounts or unlimited importing.

Choose Edgewonk when psychology tagging and specialist process analysis are the priority. Consider TraderSync or TradeZella when their particular automation, replay or coaching workflows better match the review routine. The correct comparison uses the same broker file and the same analytical question in each trial—not feature-count marketing.

Our current assessment

Trademetria offers unusually broad portfolio and asset coverage at competitive current prices. The free plan, full-feature usage trial, AI tools and extensive feature parity across tiers make it more capable than the stale page suggested.

Its main limitations are capacity gates, inconsistent import methods, asset-specific constraints and a restrictive post-payment refund policy. The strongest buyer has multiple kinds of positions and wants a trading back office. A pure high-frequency day trader should verify execution imports and order counting before committing.
