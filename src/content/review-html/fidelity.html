Fidelity is one of the strongest default brokers for long-term investors, but it is not the cleanest choice for every active trader. The decision depends on whether you value research, retirement accounts, funds and service more than advanced order routing, APIs or specialist day-trading tools.

This review was refreshed on July 21, 2026 using Fidelity's official commissions and trading-platform pages. Fidelity currently advertises $0 online US stock, ETF and options trades, with online options subject to a $0.65 per-contract fee. Fidelity also now presents Fidelity Trader+ Desktop as the rebuilt active-trading desktop platform while noting Active Trader Pro access remains available.

The pricing is excellent for ordinary investors

Fidelity's everyday investing price structure remains very competitive: $0 online US stock and ETF commissions, no retail brokerage account minimum, and access to Fidelity's zero-expense-ratio index funds. Options traders still need to include the $0.65 per-contract fee shown on Fidelity's official commissions page.

That makes Fidelity easy to recommend for buy-and-hold investors, retirement savers and research-driven stock or fund buyers. It is less automatically compelling for very active options, futures or algorithmic traders.

Research and planning are the real moat

Fidelity is strongest when the account is more than a trading ticket. Research, screeners, fund tools, retirement planning, cash-management features, fixed-income access and customer service make the platform useful for investors who manage their full financial life in one place.

That matters for users who do not want to stitch together a broker, research terminal, retirement portal and cash account. Fidelity's breadth is the product.

Active Trader Pro and Trader+ need a fresh comparison

Older reviews often focused on Active Trader Pro. Fidelity's current trading-platform page now highlights Fidelity Trader+ Desktop as a downloadable multi-monitor command center rebuilt from the legacy platform, while also saying Active Trader Pro access is not impacted by downloading Trader+.

Active traders should test the desktop platform themselves before moving serious workflow. Compare chart speed, order tickets, options chains, watchlists, multi-monitor behavior and alerts against thinkorswim, Interactive Brokers and tastytrade.

Where Fidelity is weaker

Fidelity is not built around open APIs, professional multi-asset routing or crypto-native trading. Futures access and very active trader workflows are not its strongest selling points. A trader who wants the broadest global access should compare Interactive Brokers. A trader focused on options workflow may prefer tastytrade or thinkorswim.

For the average investor, those limitations are acceptable. For a professional or systematic trader, they are central.

Our current assessment

Fidelity remains a top-tier brokerage for long-term investors and serious personal-finance users. Its combination of low core costs, research, retirement support and service is hard to beat. The review should not pretend it is the best specialist platform for every active-trading workflow. It is best when the user wants one reliable investing home, not a niche execution engine.
