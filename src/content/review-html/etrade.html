Quick verdict: E*TRADE is strongest for self-directed investors and options traders who want Power E*TRADE, Morgan Stanley research, $0 listed stock and ETF commissions, and a familiar online-brokerage workflow. It is not the deepest futures or API-first platform.

What E*TRADE is best at

E*TRADE is now part of Morgan Stanley, but the core buyer case remains familiar: a full online brokerage with strong options tools, usable web and mobile apps, research, retirement accounts, and managed-account options. The standout trading surface is Power E*TRADE, especially for options chains, strategy analysis, risk/reward views, and active monitoring.

That makes E*TRADE more serious than a simple mobile broker, but less specialized than a professional futures platform or API-first broker. If you mainly trade listed stocks, ETFs, and options, it belongs on the shortlist. If your process depends on futures scalping, custom automation, or very low margin rates, compare it carefully with Interactive Brokers, tastytrade, and Tradovate.

Current pricing details that matter

E*TRADE's public pricing page lists $0 standard commissions for online US-listed stock and ETF trades, with options at $0.65 per contract. The same pricing language says active traders who make at least 30 stock, ETF, and options trades per quarter can qualify for $0.50 options contract pricing.

For futures, E*TRADE discloses $1.50 per side, per contract, plus exchange and regulatory fees. That headline futures commission is competitive, but futures traders still need to evaluate platform fit, data, order-entry workflow, margin, product access, and whether Power E*TRADE or E*TRADE Futures matches the way they actually trade.

Platform fit

Power E*TRADE is the reason active traders still consider the platform. It gives options traders a better workflow than a basic brokerage ticket: strategy views, probability-style analysis, clearer multi-leg setup, watchlists, and charting. The standard E*TRADE experience is more useful for account management, investing, research, and longer-term portfolio work.

The Morgan Stanley connection adds research and wealth-management context. That is useful for investors who want brokerage plus planning, but it does not automatically make E*TRADE the best tool for every active trading style. Traders should separate account value from platform execution value.

Where E*TRADE is weaker

E*TRADE is not the cleanest choice for traders who need deep automation, a public API workflow, crypto trading, or the most specialized futures setup. It is also easy to overrate the platform if you only look at options commissions. Execution tools, order entry, risk controls, and data workflow matter as much as the contract fee.

For pure options education and active options workflow, compare E*TRADE against tastytrade and Schwab's thinkorswim. For broader investing and banking, compare it against Charles Schwab and Fidelity.

Who should choose E*TRADE?

  • Choose E*TRADE if you want a mature broker with strong options tools and Morgan Stanley research.
  • Use Power E*TRADE if multi-leg options analysis is central to the workflow.
  • Compare futures brokers if futures are your primary market.
  • Look elsewhere if API-first automation or crypto access is required.

Bottom line

E*TRADE remains a credible self-directed brokerage, especially for options traders who like Power E*TRADE. It is strongest as a mainstream investing and options platform, not as a specialist futures, crypto, or automation product.
