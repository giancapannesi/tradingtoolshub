Insider Data Needs Context Before It Becomes Useful

Officer and director transactions, beneficial ownership changes, institutional filings, and political disclosures follow different reporting rules and timelines. A useful tool distinguishes those datasets instead of combining every transaction into one undifferentiated signal.

Users should be able to inspect the filer, security, transaction type, ownership change, filing date, transaction date, source document, and the person's prior activity. Delayed disclosure means the data is research context, not guaranteed real-time intent.

Evaluate the Research Workflow

  • Direct links to original filings and clear correction handling.
  • Filters for role, transaction type, size, repeat activity, and clustering.
  • Alerts that explain why an event matched.
  • Company, sector, price, and historical context.
  • Exports or watchlists that support independent verification.
