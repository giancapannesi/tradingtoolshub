The Lowest Advertised Fee Is Rarely the Lowest Total Cost

Promotional prices expire, and a cheap evaluation can become expensive after resets, recurring subscriptions, activation charges, platform fees, or repeated attempts. This ranking therefore treats price as a path rather than a single checkout number.

Calculate the amount required to reach the first eligible payout under a realistic timeline. Include the evaluation, renewal or access period, failed-attempt policy, activation, data, and withdrawal conditions. Never base the decision on a temporary coupon that is not visible at checkout.

Compare Cost Against Usable Drawdown

A $50,000 headline account with a $2,000 loss limit behaves more like a tightly constrained $2,000 risk account than a conventional $50,000 brokerage balance. Divide total expected fees by usable drawdown and by the number of attempts your historical results suggest you may need.

  • One-time access versus recurring subscription.
  • End-of-day versus intraday trailing thresholds.
  • Reset, re-purchase, and funded-account activation costs.
  • Minimum days, consistency rules, and payout gates.
