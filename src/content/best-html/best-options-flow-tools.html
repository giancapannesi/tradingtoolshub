Options Flow Is Context, Not a Standalone Trade Signal

Large premium, repeated sweeps, unusual volume, and opening activity can help identify where attention is concentrated, but none of them proves direction or informed intent. A transaction may hedge stock, close an existing position, form one leg of a spread, or reflect market-making activity.

The strongest tools help users inspect the underlying contract, expiration, strike, volume versus open interest, execution price, surrounding volatility, and related stock or dark-pool context.

A Safer Flow Workflow

  1. Filter for liquid contracts and meaningful size.
  2. Check whether activity is likely opening or closing.
  3. Review the underlying chart, catalyst, liquidity, and volatility.
  4. Define risk independently; never copy premium size as position size.
  5. Journal whether the signal added information after costs and false positives.

For ownership and executive activity rather than derivatives flow, compare insider trading tools.
