Broker Head-to-Head

Fidelity and J.P. Morgan Self-Directed Investing both offer zero-commission stock and ETF trading, but target different investors. Fidelity (4.7/5) excels in research depth and analytical tools with Active Trader Pro, zero-fee index funds, and #1-ranked customer service—appealing to data-driven traders. J.P. Morgan (3.8/5) emphasizes friction-free accessibility through seamless Chase account integration, no minimum balance, and Morningstar research, designed for banking customers prioritizing convenience over advanced functionality.

Fidelity's competitive edge lies in superior research infrastructure and zero-expense-ratio index funds, enabling sophisticated analysis and lower long-term costs. J.P. Morgan differentiates through Chase ecosystem integration, eliminating switching costs for existing JPM customers and simplifying account management across banking and investing.

Choose Fidelity if you leverage backtesting, charting, and paper trading—its rating premium reflects genuine capability advantages for active traders. Select J.P. Morgan if you're a Chase customer seeking straightforward investing without learning advanced platforms; its limitations in charting and absence of futures/crypto don't affect stock-and-ETF-focused portfolios. The 0.9-point rating gap indicates Fidelity's technical depth justifies switching costs for serious traders, while J.P. Morgan's strength is serving casual investors already in the Chase ecosystem.
