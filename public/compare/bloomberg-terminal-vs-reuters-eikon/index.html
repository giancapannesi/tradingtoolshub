Market-Data Head-to-Head

Important: Refinitiv Eikon is not a current alternative you can buy. LSEG ended Eikon access after June 30, 2025. The current comparison is Bloomberg Terminal versus LSEG Workspace.

Pricing

Bloomberg Terminal is commonly sold as a premium per-user institutional service. LSEG Workspace uses quote-based pricing that varies with datasets, exchange entitlements, roles and workflow requirements. Historical Eikon estimates are not a reliable current Workspace price.

Request itemized proposals and compare the base workstation, real-time exchanges, specialist datasets, APIs, Office integrations, support and additional users. A headline monthly figure can conceal different entitlements.

Where Bloomberg is strongest

Bloomberg’s core advantage is its integrated professional ecosystem: broad market data and analytics, Bloomberg News, terminal functions and the institutional messaging network. For desks whose counterparties already communicate through Bloomberg, network effects can outweigh a feature-by-feature price comparison.

Where LSEG Workspace is strongest

Workspace combines LSEG data and analytics with Reuters News, customizable workflows, web and desktop access, mobile access, APIs and Office capabilities. It is the appropriate successor for organizations that previously evaluated or used Eikon.

Migration and integration

Teams moving from Eikon should inventory spreadsheets, COM automation, desktop-session code and old .NET integrations. LSEG documents replacements for certain legacy interfaces, while supported data libraries can continue in Workspace configurations. Migration effort belongs in the total-cost comparison.

Decision

  • Choose Bloomberg when its messaging network, established desk workflow and specific analytics are essential.
  • Choose Workspace when Reuters News, LSEG datasets, current Workspace APIs and its workflow model fit the team better.
  • Choose a specialist alternative when the organization needs only a narrow slice of institutional-terminal functionality.

Bloomberg Terminal: Pros & Cons

Pros

  • + Most comprehensive financial data source covering every asset class and geography
  • + Bloomberg News with 2,700+ journalists delivers market-moving headlines first
  • + Industry-standard messaging network essential for institutional deal flow
  • + Best-in-class fixed income, derivatives, and portfolio risk analytics
  • + Robust API for Excel, Python, and proprietary system integration
  • + Responsive 24/7 customer support staffed by knowledgeable specialists

Cons

  • - $24,000/year cost is prohibitive for retail traders and small firms
  • - Two-year standard contracts with difficult early cancellation
  • - Dated keyboard-driven interface with steep weeks-long learning curve
  • - No free tier or trial period for individual evaluation
  • - Massive feature overkill for anyone not managing institutional-scale portfolios

Refinitiv Eikon (Retired): Pros & Cons

Pros

  • + Modern successor to Eikon with desktop, browser and iOS access
  • + Exclusive Reuters News plus professional data workflows
  • + Workspace APIs and updated Office integrations
  • + Customizable institutional research and trading workflows

Cons

  • - No transparent public list price
  • - Not designed or priced for ordinary retail traders
  • - Legacy Eikon COM and .NET workflows may require migration work
  • - Data and exchange entitlements can make quotes difficult to compare
