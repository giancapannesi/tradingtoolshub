Broker Head-to-Head

E*TRADE and Webull serve different trader profiles with overlapping but distinct strengths. E*TRADE (4.3/5) positions itself as the premium options trading platform, leveraging Morgan Stanley's research capabilities and Power E*TRADE's advanced tools with volume-based commission discounts. Webull (4.2/5) targets retail traders seeking accessibility through commission-free crypto, extended trading hours (4AM-8PM ET), and built-in paper trading with live market data. Both platforms offer AI analysis, backtesting, and mobile apps, but diverge significantly in product scope and market focus.

E*TRADE's critical differentiator is its options-first architecture combined with institutional research integration—ideal for traders prioritizing sophisticated derivatives strategies. Webull counters with comprehensive asset coverage including cryptocurrency and an extended trading window that captures pre-market and after-hours volatility. E*TRADE's limitations (no crypto, restricted futures) reflect its institutional lean, while Webull's gaps (no mutual funds, slower support) reflect its retail-first design.

Options traders with higher volume or those wanting Morgan Stanley research should choose E*TRADE. Retail traders interested in cryptocurrency, pre-market positioning, or after-hours execution should pick Webull. If you need crypto access at all, Webull is mandatory—E*TRADE doesn't offer it. For serious options work, E*TRADE's specialized tools and research premium justify the platform choice.

Webull: Pros & Cons

Pros

  • + Commission-free trading across stocks, ETFs, options, and 50+ cryptos
  • + Extended hours trading 4AM-8PM ET plus overnight trading on select securities
  • + Best-in-class paper trading — ranked #1 by StockBrokers.com for 2026
  • + 56 technical indicators, order flow analysis, and Replay Mode on a free platform
  • + Webull Premium at $40/year is cheaper than Robinhood Gold and adds Level 2 + lower margin rates
  • + Fractional shares, IPO access, futures, and event contracts in one platform

Cons

  • - Customer support ranked last (0.92/10) among major brokers — hold times exceed 10 minutes
  • - PFOF accounts for ~50% of revenue, which may impact trade execution quality
  • - No SEP IRAs, inherited IRAs, trust accounts, or custodial accounts (529s/UTMAs)
  • - Crypto cannot be transferred to external wallets — coins stay on Webull
  • - Stock screener is basic compared to dedicated tools like Finviz or Trade Ideas
