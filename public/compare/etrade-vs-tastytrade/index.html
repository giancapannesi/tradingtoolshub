Broker Head-to-Head

E*TRADE and Tastytrade both offer commission-free trading but target different audiences. E*TRADE, backed by Morgan Stanley, positions itself as a full-service platform with broad asset coverage and institutional research integration. Tastytrade has optimized exclusively for options traders, building probability-based tools and risk management into every feature. E*TRADE attracts traders seeking comprehensive market access; Tastytrade appeals to specialists willing to narrow their focus for superior execution.

The key differentiator is options pricing and educational approach. Tastytrade caps options commissions at $10 per leg—industry-leading for active traders—and includes Tastylive's streaming education built into the platform. E*TRADE offers volume-based discounts on options and Morgan Stanley research integration, plus Power E*TRADE's advanced backtesting and technical analysis tools. Tastytrade acknowledges charting limitations compared to competitors, while E*TRADE's charting remains serviceable but not specialized.

Choose Tastytrade if options comprise 80%+ of your trading activity—the $10 leg cap delivers substantial savings on frequent trades. E*TRADE suits diversified traders needing stocks, ETFs, and options access with institutional research, particularly those who may explore futures or other asset classes. Both platforms score 4.3-4.5 stars with identical core features (AI analysis, backtesting, paper trading, alerts, mobile apps), making the decision primarily about trading focus and pricing structure.

Tastytrade: Pros & Cons

Pros

  • + Built by the founders of thinkorswim — Tom Sosnoff, Tony Battista, Scott Sheridan
  • + Cheapest options commissions: $1 to open, $0 to close, capped at $10/leg for large orders
  • + $0 stock and ETF commissions
  • + Platform purpose-built for options selling strategies — IV rank, probability of profit, and theta front and center
  • + tastylive media network: 8+ hours of live daily programming with data-driven options strategy analysis
  • + Follow Feed lets you see and optionally replicate trades from tastylive hosts
  • + Portfolio beta weighting shows entire portfolio exposure as a single number
  • + Well-designed mobile app with full trading capability
  • + Curve analysis with visual P&L diagrams integrated into the brokerage

Cons

  • - Basic charting compared to TradingView or thinkorswim — functional but limited
  • - No advanced scripting or custom indicator development
  • - No order flow tools — no footprint charts, volume profiles, or DOM
  • - No API for programmatic trading
  • - Limited stock research and fundamental analysis tools — options-focused platform
  • - 1% crypto trading fee is higher than dedicated crypto exchanges
