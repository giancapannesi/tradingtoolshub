Journal Head-to-Head

Short answer: choose Edgewonk if you want a structured psychology and process journal with annual billing. Choose TraderTrac if you want a lighter AI-first journal with fast capture, weekly AI reports and lower monthly commitment.

Where Edgewonk Wins

Edgewonk is strongest for traders who want a structured improvement process. It is less about being trendy and more about forcing the trader to think in terms of behavior, process, emotional state and statistical edge. The psychology focus is a real differentiator, especially for traders who already know that discipline is the problem.

The downside is billing and feel. Edgewonk is not the obvious pick if you want a modern AI assistant, a free tier, or the lightest possible entry point. It works best when a trader is ready to commit to a structured process for months, not when they are still deciding whether journaling will stick.

Where TraderTrac Wins

TraderTrac is more approachable. It has a free tier, a cheaper Pro plan, AI psychology review, weekly reports and paid IBKR auto-import through SnapTrade. That makes it a better fit for traders who want to build a review habit without buying into a heavier annual workflow first.

TraderTrac's AI angle is also more direct. Instead of only logging metrics, the product is positioned around surfacing repeated mistakes, psychology patterns and rule issues. That matters for traders who need plain-language feedback from their journal.

Bottom Line

Edgewonk is a good fit for traders who want structure and psychology depth. TraderTrac is the better fit for traders who want AI feedback, easier adoption and lower subscription risk. The practical decision is whether you need a disciplined framework or a faster AI-assisted logging workflow.
