Broker Head-to-Head

## Overview

Charles Schwab and TD Ameritrade both offer commission-free trading, powerful platforms, and extensive research tools. However, a critical distinction defines this comparison: Schwab is the active platform accepting new customers today, while TD Ameritrade merged into Schwab in 2020 and no longer opens new accounts. If you're choosing between them in 2026, you're deciding whether to open with Schwab, migrate an existing TD Ameritrade account, or evaluate legacy accounts against current offerings.

## Pricing Comparison

Both platforms are commission-free for stock and ETF trades—there's no account minimum for either. The actual cost difference comes from options and derivatives trading. Charles Schwab and TD Ameritrade both charge $0.65 per options contract, meaning a 10-contract trade costs $6.50 on either platform. Schwab charges higher futures commissions than some competitors (approximately $2.20 per contract round-trip for standard markets), while legacy TD Ameritrade accounts have comparable rates. Neither platform charges account opening, maintenance, or withdrawal fees. Both offer paper trading accounts at no cost, making them equally accessible for learning. The pricing verdict is a draw: if you trade only stocks and ETFs, you'll pay nothing on either platform; options and futures traders face identical per-contract costs.

## Key Features Head-to-Head

**Trading Platform:** Both use thinkorSwim, an industry-leading desktop and mobile platform with advanced charting, backtesting, and strategy analysis. Schwab's version is actively updated and integrated with their ecosystem; TD Ameritrade's thinkorSwim receives maintenance-level updates. Edge: Schwab for ongoing development.

**Research and Analysis Tools:** Schwab integrates Morningstar research, FactSet data, and custom screening tools across their ecosystem. TD Ameritrade offers comparable research depth through legacy partnerships, but integration with new Schwab infrastructure is incomplete. Edge: Schwab for seamless integration.

**API Access:** Both provide API access for algorithmic trading and custom applications. Schwab's API is actively modernized; TD Ameritrade's API works but operates on legacy infrastructure. Edge: Schwab for developers building new systems.

**Wealth Management Services:** Schwab offers advisory accounts, portfolio management, and banking services (checking, savings) all in one ecosystem. TD Ameritrade offers advisory services but not integrated banking. Edge: Schwab for buy-it-all-in-one-place customers.

**Options Trading Capabilities:** TD Ameritrade has historically offered competitive options screening and strategy tools, though Schwab's integration is catching up. Both platforms support all options strategies equally. Edge: Tie (slight legacy advantage for TD power options traders).

**Account Migration Support:** Schwab actively assists migrants from TD Ameritrade; TD Ameritrade supports outbound transfers. Edge: Schwab for current customer experience.

## Who Should Choose Charles Schwab

- **New traders and investors opening accounts in 2026**: Schwab is the only option accepting new applicants; there's no genuine choice here for most people. - **Active traders combining stocks, options, and some futures**: Schwab's unified platform, thinkorSwim access, and integrated research create a complete toolkit without switching between systems. - **Investors wanting integrated banking and wealth management**: If you want trading, margin accounts, advisory services, and checking accounts in one interface, Schwab delivers this seamlessly. TD Ameritrade never offered banking integration. - **Portfolio managers requiring API access for automation**: Schwab's actively maintained API infrastructure supports algorithmic trading systems better than TD Ameritrade's legacy setup.

## Who Should Choose TD Ameritrade

- **Existing account holders avoiding forced migration**: If you have an established TD Ameritrade account with years of history, customized watchlists, and trading patterns, staying put avoids Schwab's transition friction. - **Options specialists comfortable with legacy systems**: TD Ameritrade's options infrastructure is mature and stable; if your strategies work there, migration creates unnecessary risk. - **Traders locked into third-party integrations**: If your trading software or data feeds connect specifically to TD Ameritrade's API, switching to Schwab requires re-engineering integrations. - **Accounts with unique legacy features or rates**: Some old TD Ameritrade accounts have grandfathered terms (lower options fees, specific margin rates) not available on new Schwab accounts—keeping that account preserves those advantages.

## The Verdict

For anyone opening a brokerage account in 2026, Charles Schwab is the clear choice: it's the only platform accepting new applicants, offers identical commission-free trading, provides the same thinkorSwim platform, and adds integrated banking and wealth management that TD Ameritrade never developed. For existing TD Ameritrade customers, migration to Schwab makes sense unless your account includes grandfathered benefits or you rely on integrations Schwab hasn't yet replicated. The merger strongly favors Schwab's vision—it's the active platform, while TD Ameritrade functions as a legacy name gradually phasing out.
