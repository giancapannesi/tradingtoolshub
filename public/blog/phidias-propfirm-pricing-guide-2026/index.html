Phidias PropFirm Pricing Explained: All Plans, Costs & Fees (2026)

Complete breakdown of Phidias PropFirm pricing — all plans, hidden fees, and how to save money.

By TradingToolsHub Editorial Published April 5, 2026
Product details cited here reflect the vendor's public documentation at time of writing. Always confirm current pricing before signing up. Editorial process →

Phidias PropFirm Pricing Overview

Phidias PropFirm is a Gibraltar-based futures prop firm that stands out for its one-time evaluation fee model rather than ongoing monthly subscriptions. Launched in 2023 by French traders, the firm targets futures day traders and scalpers who want to avoid recurring costs while accessing funded accounts with up to 90% profit splits.

The entry point starts at $55 per month for the Static 25K plan—one of the most affordable prop firm options on the market. However, it's important to understand that Phidias charges a one-time evaluation fee upfront, after which your monthly costs remain locked in with no hidden subscription increases, data feed charges, or activation fees.

All Phidias PropFirm Plans Compared

Phidias PropFirm offers four distinct pricing tiers, each designed for different account sizes and trading styles. Here's the complete breakdown:

Plan Name Account Size Monthly Cost Annual Cost Best For
Static 25K $25,000 $55 $660 Beginners, part-time traders testing the platform
Fundamental 50K $50,000 $116 $1,392 Active day traders, small account scalpers
Fundamental 100K $100,000 $145 $1,740 Intermediate traders, swing traders on futures
Fundamental 150K $150,000 $173 $2,076 Professional traders, multiple account holders

Each plan comes with access to Phidias' proprietary trading platform, integrated with Rithmic technology, along with performance analytics dashboards and risk management tools. The pricing is straightforward: you pay one evaluation fee upfront, then the monthly cost above covers platform access and account management.

What makes Phidias unique is the EOD (end-of-day) drawdown calculation model. Unlike firms that calculate drawdowns intraday, Phidias only counts losses that exist at market close. This means if you lose $500 during the trading day but recover $400 by close, only the $100 net loss counts against your drawdown limit—a significant advantage for scalpers and intraday traders.

Free Plan / Free Trial

Phidias PropFirm does not offer a free tier or free trial. To access a funded account, you must pay the one-time evaluation fee first. However, this is actually a advantage compared to firms that charge recurring fees: once you've passed evaluation and funded an account, there are no monthly subscription costs—only the per-month maintenance charge listed above.

The lack of a free trial is offset by the firm's affordable entry price and transparent fee structure. Many traders view the one-time evaluation cost as a reasonable barrier that filters out casual traders, ensuring a community of serious professionals.

Hidden Costs and Fees

One of Phidias PropFirm's biggest selling points is the absence of hidden fees. The firm explicitly advertises:

  • No activation fees after evaluation
  • No data feed charges (Rithmic data is included)
  • No monthly subscriptions beyond the platform fee
  • No payout caps on Live accounts
  • Daily withdrawals available via Phidias Wallet

However, there are some important caveats to be aware of:

  • CASH accounts have monthly payout caps until you reach the $75K threshold. This restricts earnings potential for traders still building their track record.
  • Futures contract fees and exchange fees are built into the spread/trading costs—these are standard across all prop firms but should be factored into your profitability calculations.
  • Rithmic platform costs may apply if you use features beyond the included access, though this is rare for standard trading.
  • Withdrawal fees via Phidias Wallet are minimal but should be confirmed before opening an account.

Compared to competitors, Phidias is genuinely transparent about costs. Most prop firms bury data feed costs or platform upgrades in fine print—Phidias lists everything upfront.

The 90% profit split is also industry-leading, and 1–4 hour payouts via Phidias Wallet beat most competitors' 24–48 hour timelines.

The tradeoff: Phidias is futures-only. If you trade spot forex, stocks, or crypto, firms like Apex or FTMO offer broader market access. Phidias' focus on futures is actually an advantage for scalpers and day traders but limits flexibility.

Is Phidias PropFirm Worth the Price?

Value assessment: 8.5/10 for futures traders, 4/10 for diversified traders.

Phidias delivers exceptional value if you meet two criteria:

  • You trade futures exclusively (micro contracts, ES, YM, NQ, CL, GC, etc.)
  • You're a day trader or scalper who benefits from EOD drawdown rules and quick payouts

For these traders, Phidias offers the lowest monthly costs, highest profit splits, and fastest payouts in the industry. The one-time evaluation fee model also appeals to traders who want to avoid monthly recurring charges and prefer paying as they trade.

When each plan makes sense:

  • Static 25K ($55/mo): Best for beginners testing the platform and part-time traders. At $660/year, it's a low-risk entry point.
  • Fundamental 50K ($116/mo): The sweet spot for active day traders. The 2x account size vs. Static 25K costs only 2.1x more, making it excellent value.
  • Fundamental 100K ($145/mo): Ideal if you're trading 2–3 contracts per trade or managing multiple positions simultaneously.
  • Fundamental 150K ($173/mo): For professional traders running multiple accounts or trading larger sizes. The monthly cost is competitive for the account size.

When should you upgrade? If your average daily losses exceed 50% of your monthly fee, upgrading pays for itself immediately. For example, if you're on the 50K plan ($116/mo) and consistently lose $200+ per day, upgrading to 100K ($145/mo) gives you more breathing room and typically unlocks better profit split rates on Live accounts.

How to Save on Phidias PropFirm

Phidias PropFirm pricing is already lean, but here are legitimate ways to reduce costs:

  • Start with Static 25K. At $55/month ($660/year), it's the lowest entry point. Once you've proven profitability, upgrade to a larger account rather than starting big.
  • Lock in annual billing. Pay 12 months upfront to save on recurring charges (if available—confirm with Phidias directly).
  • Avoid payout caps by reaching $75K on Live accounts. This unlocks unlimited daily withdrawals and removes the cash account restrictions.
  • Maximize the 90% profit split. On Live accounts, you keep up to 90% of profits. Focus on consistency rather than account size—a $50K account with 10% monthly returns beats a $150K account with inconsistent results.
  • Use news trading strategies. Phidias allows news trading on all account types, unlike some competitors. This can reduce costs by shortening hold times and increasing winning trades.

Unlike some prop firms, Phidias doesn't offer promotional discount codes or student plans. The pricing is fixed and fair, with no artificial discounts that mask higher true costs.

Bottom line: Phidias PropFirm pricing is transparent, competitive, and genuinely low for futures traders. The combination of sub-$200/month costs, up to 90% profit splits, EOD drawdown rules, and 1–4 hour payouts makes it one of the best-value prop firms for the futures-focused trader. Just ensure futures trading aligns with your strategy before committing.
