Goat Funded Trader Rules Explained: Drawdown, Profit Targets & Time Limits (2026)

Goat Funded Trader Rules Overview

Goat Funded Trader is a proprietary trading firm founded in 2023 that operates out of Hong Kong (Wishes Tower International Limited). The firm specializes in challenges and evaluations for forex, indices, metals, commodities, stocks, and cryptocurrencies, with support for 500+ crypto CFDs—one of the broadest crypto selections in the prop trading industry.

Unlike traditional prop firms with single evaluation paths, Goat Funded Trader offers six distinct challenge models, including an ultra-low entry option ($1) and instant funding pathways. The firm's standout feature is its no time limit policy on most evaluations—traders need only complete a minimum of 3 trading days before becoming eligible to advance or receive payouts. Successful traders can scale to $2M in simulated capital with 100% profit splits and on-demand withdrawals at the top tier.

The firm accepts multiple payment methods, including cryptocurrency, and regularly runs discount promotions up to 50% off challenge fees.

Profit Targets

Goat Funded Trader employs a two-phase evaluation structure for its 2-Step accounts (the most common challenge type). Each phase has distinct profit targets that traders must exceed to progress.

Phase 1 Profit Target: Specific percentage targets are not detailed in publicly available summaries, but typically align with industry standards of 5-10% on lower-tier accounts, with potential variation by account size.

Phase 2 Profit Target: The second phase generally features a lower profit requirement than Phase 1, incentivizing completion of the evaluation process.

Profit Calculation:

  • Profits are calculated on net P&L against the starting account balance
  • Profit targets must be met while staying within maximum drawdown limits
  • Targets reset at the beginning of each phase
  • Trades closed at a loss reduce total profit and extend the timeline to reach targets

Instant Goat Difference: The Instant Goat $100K account bypasses the evaluation process entirely—traders pay the $899 monthly fee and receive immediate funding without hitting profit targets. This model suits traders who already pass internal vetting or prefer to skip the challenge phase.

Drawdown Rules

Drawdown management is critical to the evaluation process. Goat Funded Trader enforces both daily and maximum drawdown limits to protect the integrity of the challenge and ensure disciplined risk management.

Daily Drawdown Limit: A maximum daily loss threshold prevents catastrophic single-day losses. The exact percentage is not specified in standard public data but typically ranges from 2-5% of account balance depending on tier.

Maximum Drawdown (Trailing): The firm employs a trailing drawdown system, meaning the maximum loss is measured from the highest peak of equity reached during the trading period, not from the starting balance. This allows traders to recover from earlier losses if they generate new highs.

Example Scenario:

  • Starting balance: $25,000
  • Peak equity reached: $26,500 (after initial profitable trades)
  • Current equity: $24,925 (a $1,575 drawdown from the peak)
  • The drawdown is calculated from $26,500, not $25,000
  • If maximum drawdown is 5%, the threshold would be $25,175; the account would be at risk of termination

Drawdown Reset Conditions:

  • Drawdown does NOT reset during the evaluation period—traders must manage it throughout
  • Once an account is funded and phases are completed, drawdown rules may adjust (specific terms vary by funded tier)
  • Minimum 3 trading days required before eligibility; drawdown applies from day one

No Time Limit Advantage: Unlike competitors with strict 30-60 day windows, Goat Funded Trader's no-time-limit policy allows traders to trade around the drawdown constraint indefinitely, as long as they respect the 3-day minimum activity requirement.

Trading Restrictions

Goat Funded Trader enforces several trading restrictions designed to prevent arbitrage, excessive risk, and strategy gaming:

Time-Based Restrictions:

  • Minimum 3 trading days required before evaluation completion
  • No maximum time limit on evaluations (traders can hold accounts indefinitely)
  • Weekend holdings are permitted on applicable markets (forex, commodities)

News Trading Rules:

  • News trading is permitted but capped at 1% profit per trade
  • This restriction limits the profitability of event-driven strategies around high-impact news releases (NFP, central bank decisions, etc.)
  • Scalping news spikes is effectively restricted despite the ability to trade through news

Instrument and Lot Size Limits:

  • All major forex pairs, indices, metals, and commodities are available
  • 500+ crypto CFDs supported (one of the broadest crypto selections)
  • Specific lot size limits are not publicly detailed but typically scale with account size
  • Leverage terms are not specified in available public summaries

Automated Trading and Expert Advisors:

  • API access is supported as a key feature
  • Automated trading and expert advisors (EAs) are permitted on desktop platforms
  • Copy trading restrictions (if any) are not detailed in standard rule summaries

Platform Requirements:

  • Mobile app is available but analytics-only—traders cannot execute trades via mobile
  • Trade execution requires a separate desktop platform (specific platforms not listed in base data)
  • This dual-platform approach separates account monitoring from execution

Profit Split and Payouts

One of Goat Funded Trader's most competitive features is its profit-sharing structure, which improves as traders scale through stages.

Profit Split by Stage:

  • Phase 1 & 2 (Evaluation): Profit split percentage during the challenge phase is not explicitly stated in available summaries. Typically, firms retain 20-30% of profits during evaluation.
  • Stage 4 (Top Tier): Reaches 100% profit split, meaning funded traders keep all profits generated
  • Mid-stage splits (Stages 1-3) progressively increase but specific percentages vary by tier

Payout Frequency and Minimums:

  • Stage 4 accounts feature on-demand payouts, allowing withdrawals whenever traders choose
  • Lower-stage payout terms are not detailed in standard summaries but typically involve monthly or bi-weekly cycles
  • Minimum withdrawal amounts are not specified in provided data

Payout Processing:

  • Processing time is not detailed in standard rule summaries
  • Multiple withdrawal methods likely available (bank transfer, crypto, etc.) based on account funding options
  • Cryptocurrency payment acceptance suggests crypto withdrawals may be available

Funded Account Costs:

  • High-tier $899/month Instant Goat accounts require ongoing payment

Scaling Plan

Goat Funded Trader's scaling structure is progressive, allowing traders to increase simulated capital and improve profit splits as they demonstrate consistent trading performance.

Stage Structure Overview:

  • Stage 1: Starting account (evaluation phase 1)
  • Stage 2: Evaluation phase 2
  • Stage 3: First scaling tier (specific capital not detailed in base data)
  • Stage 4: Maximum scaling ($2M simulated capital, 100% profit split, on-demand payouts)

Scaling Requirements:

  • Traders must meet profit targets for each phase to advance
  • Drawdown limits must be respected throughout
  • No time limit on progression (traders can trade indefinitely to reach each stage)
  • At least 3 trading days minimum activity per stage

Instant Goat Alternative:

  • Traders unwilling to complete evaluation can pay $899/month for immediate $100K funding
  • Scaling beyond $100K from Instant Goat accounts is not detailed in available summaries
  • This path suits traders with proven track records or existing prop firm experience

Timeline: Specific progression timeline (e.g., how many months between stages) is not provided in standard rule summaries. The no-time-limit policy suggests traders can scale at their own pace once earning criteria are met.

Red Flags and Fine Print

Before opening a Goat Funded Trader account, traders should be aware of these potential concerns:

Instant Goat $100K—No Refund Policy:

  • If an Instant Goat account fails (violates drawdown rules or other terms), the $899 monthly fee is non-refundable
  • Unlike the 2-Step challenges where traders only pay per-month, Instant Goat carries higher upfront risk
  • Each month of trading on Instant Goat is essentially a sunk cost if the account is terminated

New Company with Limited Track Record:

  • Founded in 2023; only 2-3 years of operational history compared to 5-10+ year competitors
  • Limited long-term data on trader success rates, payout reliability, or platform stability
  • Newer firms are at higher risk of regulatory changes, operational issues, or closure

News Trading Cap (1%):

  • While news trading is allowed, the 1% profit per trade cap severely limits the profitability of event-driven strategies
  • Traders relying on news catalysts (NFP, earnings, Fed announcements) will find this restrictive
  • This rule effectively penalizes high-conviction event trades

Mobile App Limitations:

  • The mobile app is analytics-only; traders cannot execute trades from their phone
  • Requires carrying a separate desktop platform for trading, which can be inconvenient for on-the-go traders
  • Competitors offer full mobile execution, making Goat less flexible for traders trading from mobile devices

Subscription Model (Not One-Time Fee):

  • All accounts are monthly subscriptions; fees continue even after passing evaluation and receiving funding
  • A $499/month 2-Step $100K account costs $5,988 annually, even with consistent profitable trading
  • Over time, subscription costs accumulate significantly compared to one-time evaluation fee competitors

Leverage and Margin Not Detailed:

  • Specific leverage limits are not provided in standard rule summaries
  • Traders should verify leverage terms directly with the firm before opening accounts

Regulated Status Unclear:
