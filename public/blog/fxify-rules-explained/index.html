FXIFY Rules Overview

FXIFY is a broker-backed proprietary trading firm founded in 2023 and headquartered in London. Unlike purely simulated evaluation platforms, FXIFY operates with real brokerage infrastructure through its partnership with FXPIG, a regulated forex and CFD broker. This model means traders are evaluated on real market conditions rather than artificial simulations.

The firm specializes in multi-strategy support, accommodating algorithmic traders, EA-based systems, crypto CFD traders, and traditional discretionary strategies. FXIFY offers four distinct evaluation pathways:

  • 3-Step Challenge: Progressive evaluation with multiple phases ($59/month)
  • 1-Step Challenge: Fast-track single evaluation ($75/month)
  • 2-Step Challenge: Mid-level two-phase evaluation ($99/month)
  • Instant Funding: Skip evaluation entirely ($899/month)

FXIFY positions itself between budget-friendly evaluation firms and premium instant-funding platforms, with the added benefit of broker-backed trading conditions and access to 300+ tradeable instruments including forex, crypto CFDs (80+), indices, commodities, and futures.

Drawdown Rules

Drawdown limits protect FXIFY's capital by ensuring traders manage risk responsibly. FXIFY enforces two primary drawdown restrictions:

  • Daily Drawdown Limit: Maximum loss allowed in a single trading day. Exceeding this terminates trading for that day.
  • Maximum (Trailing) Drawdown: Overall loss from the account's peak equity. This prevents catastrophic capital erosion across multiple days.

Calculation: Drawdown is calculated as the percentage loss from your highest account equity (peak). For example, if your account reaches $11,000 and then drops to $9,900, that represents a 10% drawdown from peak.

Reset Conditions: Daily drawdown limits typically reset at market open (usually based on MT4/MT5 server time, typically 00:00 GMT). Maximum drawdown does not reset—it's evaluated cumulatively throughout your evaluation or trading period.

Specific Limits: FXIFY's exact daily and maximum drawdown percentages are not specified in public documentation. Review the firm's terms of service for precise limits, as they may differ between challenge types (3-Step vs. 1-Step) and account tiers.

Weekend Risk: Positions held over weekends carry additional risk due to gap risk and weekend global news. Most prop firms restrict or penalize large weekend holdings. Verify FXIFY's specific weekend holding rules before carrying positions across Friday close.

Trading Restrictions

FXIFY enforces specific trading rules to ensure fair evaluation and risk management:

  • Supported Strategies: FXIFY explicitly supports scalping, day trading, swing trading, grid trading, martingale systems, and news trading—offering flexibility for diverse trader preferences.
  • Expert Advisors (EAs): Fully supported. Algorithmic traders can deploy EAs and automated systems on MT4/MT5 without restriction.
  • Copy Trading: Typically restricted or prohibited. Traders must execute their own strategies.
  • Instruments: Access to 300+ instruments (forex, crypto CFDs, indices, commodities, futures). Certain instruments may be excluded during news events or high volatility.
  • Lot Size Limits: Maximum position sizes are enforced to prevent excessive risk concentration. Limits vary by instrument and account type but are typically based on account equity.
  • News Trading: Explicitly allowed. Traders can trade during economic announcements and news events.
  • Holding Period Restrictions: No evidence-based restrictions on swing trade durations. Positions can be held for hours, days, or weeks provided drawdown rules are maintained.
  • Platform Requirements: Trading must occur on FXIFY's supported platforms (MT4, MT5, or DXTrade). Web-based and mobile trading are available, but there is no dedicated mobile app—traders must use platform-native mobile clients.

Scalping Frequency: No documented restrictions on trade frequency. Scalpers trading 20+ times daily are permissible provided rules are maintained.

Red Flags and Fine Print

Before committing to FXIFY, traders should be aware of the following considerations:

  • Limited Track Record: Founded in 2023, FXIFY has not survived a full market cycle. Long-term reliability and payout consistency are unproven. Early-stage firms occasionally shut down or revise terms unfavorably.
  • Hidden Costs: Base pricing is competitive, but optional add-ons (90% split, bi-weekly payouts, leverage boosts) can increase effective costs by 25–50%. Calculate your total monthly expense before signing up.
  • No Dedicated Mobile App: Trading relies on MT4/MT5 or DXTrade mobile versions. Some traders find third-party mobile platforms less stable than proprietary apps.
  • Instant Funding Pricing: At $899/month, Instant Funding is significantly more expensive than challenges. Only viable if you're confident you'll generate $3,000+ monthly profit (to break even within 3 months).
  • Broker Dependency: FXIFY's real broker integration (FXPIG) is an advantage but also a dependency. If FXPIG faces regulatory issues, FXIFY's operations could be affected.
  • Monthly Subscription Model: Subscription continues monthly unless cancelled. Ensure you understand auto-renewal terms to avoid unexpected charges.
  • Rule Enforcement Ambiguity: Exact drawdown percentages, profit target thresholds, and scaling timelines are not publicly specified. Rules may be more strict than disclosed once you join.
  • Profit Split Ambiguity: Advertised percentage splits may apply only to base tiers. Traders on lower capital or newer accounts might receive lower percentages.
