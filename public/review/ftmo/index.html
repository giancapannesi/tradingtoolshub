FTMO Overview

What Is FTMO?

FTMO is a proprietary trading firm — commonly called a "prop firm" — founded in 2015 and headquartered in Prague, Czech Republic. Operated by FTMO s.r.o., the company lets traders prove their skills through an evaluation process and then trade a funded demo account with the potential to earn real money. With over 3.5 million customers across 140+ countries and more than $500 million paid out in rewards, FTMO is arguably the most established name in the retail prop firm space.

But "established" doesn't mean perfect. Here's what actually matters.

How FTMO Works

FTMO operates on an evaluation model. You pay a one-time fee, pass their trading objectives, and receive a funded account. Here's the process:

2-Step Evaluation (Classic): Phase 1 requires hitting a 10% profit target within 30 days. Phase 2 drops to a 5% target with 60 days. Both phases enforce a 10% maximum loss and 5% daily loss limit. Pass both, and you get your FTMO Account.

1-Step Challenge (New): FTMO recently introduced a single-phase evaluation with a 10% max loss limit. The streamlined process removes the waiting between phases. This is a direct response to competitors like Apex Trader Funding and Topstep who have offered simpler evaluations for years.

One critical detail that many reviews gloss over: FTMO accounts are simulated. You are trading on a demo environment, not live markets. The company pays you based on your demo performance. Their website explicitly says "simulated platform" and "demo trading." This doesn't mean the payouts aren't real — they are — but you should understand the model before signing up.

Core Features

FTMO offers more than just a funded account. Here's what you actually get:

Trading platforms: MetaTrader 4, MetaTrader 5, cTrader, and DXtrade. Four solid options covering virtually every trading style and automation setup.

Up to $200,000 in simulated capital across forex, indices, commodities, stocks, and crypto. The market coverage is broader than most prop firms.

90% profit split — among the highest in the industry and applied cleanly without hidden conditions or tiered structures.

Biweekly payouts via bank transfer, Skrill, or crypto. Most prop firms pay monthly at best.

Performance coaches assigned to help you improve, plus a full App Suite with account analysis, equity curve breakdowns, and built-in trading journaling tools. These analytics rival standalone trading journals, which is rare for a prop firm.

Automated trading is fully permitted. You can run Expert Advisors and algorithmic strategies without restrictions.

Scaling Plan and Premium Programme let top performers grow their account sizes and unlock additional perks based on consistent profitability.

The App Suite is a genuine differentiator. Most prop firms give you a dashboard and nothing else. FTMO provides statistical breakdowns, trade-by-trade analytics, and journaling tools that actually help you identify patterns in your trading.

Pricing Breakdown

FTMO charges a one-time fee per challenge attempt. There are no subscriptions or monthly charges:

- $10,000 account: $155
- $25,000 account: $250
- $50,000 account: $345
- $100,000 account: $540
- $200,000 account: $1,080

The fee is refunded with your first payout if you pass. If you fail, the fee is gone. No refunds, no exceptions. This is the business model — FTMO makes most of its revenue from failed challenges, not from profitable traders. Understanding this incentive structure matters when evaluating any prop firm.

The Free Trial lets you test the exact same rules on a $10,000 demo account for 14 days. You can retake it unlimited times. This is genuinely useful and the single most honest thing FTMO offers. Use it before spending a dollar.

Compared to competitors: Apex Trader Funding starts at $147/month for a $25K evaluation. Topstep's $50K combine runs $49/month. Those are subscription-based — you pay monthly until you pass. FTMO's one-time fee model is cheaper if you pass quickly, but significantly more expensive if you need multiple attempts at $345-$540 per try.

What FTMO Does Well

Industry credibility is unmatched. FTMO has been operating since 2015. In an industry plagued by fly-by-night firms that disappear with traders' fees, a decade of continuous operation and $500M+ in verified payouts means something real.

The 90% profit split is straightforward. Many prop firms advertise high splits but add hidden conditions or reduce them after initial payouts. FTMO's 90/10 split is clean.

The trading tools are actually good. The performance analytics, journaling, and account metrics aren't marketing fluff. They are built to help you improve, and they work well enough that you might not need a separate trading journal.

Customer support is multilingual and responsive. Support in 20 languages via live chat, email, and WhatsApp. Most traders report reasonable response times, which is more than you can say for many competitors.

Flexibility in trading style. Scalpers, swing traders, day traders, and algo traders are all welcome. No restrictions on strategy type, and the platform diversity (MT4, MT5, cTrader, DXtrade) supports virtually any workflow.

Where FTMO Falls Short

The failure rate is brutal. Industry estimates suggest 85-95% of traders fail prop firm challenges. FTMO doesn't publish their pass rate, which tells you something. At $345 for a $50K challenge, three failed attempts cost you over $1,000 with nothing to show for it.

Strict drawdown rules catch good traders. The 5% daily drawdown and 10% max drawdown are tight. A single bad day can end your challenge even if your overall strategy is profitable over months. These rules protect FTMO's business model, not your trading career.

Non-refundable fees add up fast. Unlike subscription models where you can pause, every failed FTMO attempt is a sunk cost. Traders commonly spend $1,000-$3,000 in challenge fees before either passing or giving up.

Weekend holding restrictions on some account types. If you are a swing trader who relies on multi-day holds across weekends, check the specific rules for your account type before signing up.

It is simulated trading, not real market access. Your trades never hit real markets. While payouts are real, some experienced traders find this distinction important — particularly because the firm's financial interests are not directly aligned with your trading success.

Customer complaints about rule enforcement. Browse Trustpilot and trading forums and you will find complaints about accounts terminated for technical violations that traders felt were unfair. The rules are strictly enforced and edge cases consistently go in FTMO's favor.

Who Should Use FTMO (And Who Shouldn't)

FTMO is a good fit for:
- Forex and multi-asset traders with a proven, back-tested strategy who need capital
- Disciplined traders who can handle strict risk management rules without tilting
- Traders who want the security of the most established prop firm in the industry
- Algo traders who want to run EAs on a funded account across multiple platforms
- Anyone willing to use the Free Trial extensively before committing money

FTMO is NOT for:
- Beginners who haven't developed a consistent strategy yet — you will burn through fees and fund FTMO's bottom line
- Traders who struggle with the psychological pressure of hard drawdown limits
- Anyone expecting to trade real markets with real capital
- Traders who want lenient drawdown rules — look at Apex Trader Funding instead

How FTMO Compares to Competitors

FTMO vs. Topstep: Topstep focuses on futures with a lower monthly entry point ($49/month for a $50K combine). If you trade futures exclusively, Topstep is cheaper to get started. But FTMO offers broader market access (forex, stocks, crypto) plus a higher guaranteed profit split at 90%. FTMO's one-time fee model also means no recurring charges draining your account while you prepare.

FTMO vs. Apex Trader Funding: Apex offers simpler one-step evaluations and more lenient trailing drawdown rules, making it statistically easier to pass. Their pricing is subscription-based starting at $147/month. For traders who want the easiest path to funding, Apex wins on accessibility. For traders who want the most established firm with the best analytics tools and highest credibility, FTMO wins on trust.

The Bottom Line

FTMO earned its reputation as the industry standard for prop trading firms. The 90% profit split, robust trading tools, decade-long track record, and over $500 million in verified payouts put it ahead of most competitors on credibility alone. The Free Trial is genuinely useful — take it seriously before spending anything.

But honesty demands this: FTMO's business model profits from trader failure. The strict rules, non-refundable fees, and high failure rates mean most people who try FTMO will lose money. If you do not already have a profitable, back-tested strategy with solid risk management, you are funding FTMO's revenue, not building your own.

For experienced traders who need capital and can handle the pressure, FTMO remains the safest bet in an industry full of questionable operators. For everyone else, use the Free Trial relentlessly, practice until your strategy is bulletproof, and only then put real money on the line.
