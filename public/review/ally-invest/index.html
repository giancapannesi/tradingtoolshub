Ally Invest Overview

Ally Invest is the brokerage arm of Ally Financial, one of the largest digital financial services companies in the US. Originally built on the TradeKing platform acquired in 2016, Ally Invest offers self-directed trading with $0 commissions on stocks and ETFs, making it a competitive choice for cost-conscious investors who also want seamless integration with Ally Bank savings and checking accounts.

The platform offers three distinct services: Self-Directed Trading for active investors, Robo Portfolios for hands-off automated investing (with a cash-enhanced option that charges no advisory fee), and Managed Portfolios with a 0.30% annual advisory fee. This tiered approach lets investors shift between strategies as their needs evolve, all within a single login.

Options traders pay $0.50 per contract with no base commission, which is competitive but not the cheapest in the market. The platform supports stocks, ETFs, options, mutual funds, bonds, and forex. Notably absent are futures and cryptocurrency trading, which limits appeal for traders who want a single platform for all asset classes.

Ally Invest's biggest strength is its ecosystem integration — instant transfers between Ally Bank and brokerage accounts, no account minimums for self-directed trading, and a clean mobile app. However, the trading platform lacks advanced charting, screening tools, and research depth compared to heavyweights like Fidelity or Schwab, making it better suited to casual investors than active traders.
