Polygon.io Overview

Quick verdict: Polygon.io is best treated as market-data infrastructure, not a charting platform. Its free plan is useful for testing; the $29 and $79 stock plans remain delayed; and the $199 Advanced stock plan is the first individual tier with real-time US stock data.

Polygon.io pricing at a glance

The stock API has four individual-use tiers. Basic is free with five API calls per minute and two years of history. Starter costs $29 per month and adds unlimited calls, five years of history, WebSockets and snapshots, but its stock data is delayed by 15 minutes. Developer costs $79 per month, extends history to ten years and adds second aggregates and trades. Advanced costs $199 per month and adds real-time stock data, more than 20 years of history and quotes.

This distinction matters because “unlimited API calls” does not mean “real-time data.” Starter and Developer remove the small request ceiling while retaining delayed stock data. Buyers building live alerts or execution-adjacent dashboards should budget around the Advanced tier or obtain business pricing.

Is Polygon.io free?

Yes, but the free plan is primarily a development sandbox. Five calls per minute can support documentation examples, occasional end-of-day pulls and a small prototype. It is not enough for repeatedly scanning a large watchlist. The two-year history limit also makes it less suitable for long-horizon backtests.

A sensible workflow is to validate symbols, endpoints, pagination and response formats on Basic, then upgrade only after measuring the required asset class, history depth, call volume and latency.

What the API does well

Polygon.io provides standardized REST endpoints, WebSocket streams and downloadable flat files. Its US stock coverage includes exchanges, off-exchange activity and regulated Securities Information Processor feeds. It also provides reference data, corporate actions, fundamentals, news and technical indicators.

The platform is strongest for developers building screeners, research dashboards, alerts, backtesting pipelines and fintech products. A discretionary trader who simply wants charts, watchlists and order entry will usually get more immediate value from a charting platform.

The buying decision should start with what will consume the data. A simple watchlist script can live on delayed data. A live alerting product needs real-time streaming. A backtest needs history depth and clean corporate-action handling. A commercial product needs redistribution rights, support expectations and a business agreement before it can safely ship to customers.

Stocks versus options and other assets

Polygon.io sells access by market rather than as one universal data bundle. Stock-plan pricing should not be assumed to include the options data needed for an options scanner. The options API has its own coverage and entitlement considerations, including consolidated OPRA data. Define the markets you need before comparing headline prices.

Polygon.io versus Alpha Vantage

Alpha Vantage is easier for small experiments that benefit from pre-computed indicators, while Polygon.io offers a clearer path to WebSocket streaming and deeper production-oriented stock infrastructure. Alpha Vantage’s free quota is measured per day; Polygon.io’s free stock quota is measured per minute.

For a side-by-side decision, see Alpha Vantage versus Polygon.io. Choose based on latency, history, asset class and redistribution rights—not simply the cheapest monthly number.

Who should choose Polygon.io?

  • Choose it if you can build around APIs and need structured market data, WebSockets or downloadable files.
  • Start free if you are validating an idea and can work within five calls per minute.
  • Consider Advanced if real-time US stock data is essential for an individual-use project.
  • Look elsewhere if you want a ready-made trading terminal, native backtesting interface or broker execution workflow.

Implementation checks before paying

  1. List the exact markets you need: stocks, options, forex, crypto or indices.
  2. Confirm whether delayed data is acceptable for the workflow.
  3. Measure calls per minute against your watchlist size, refresh interval and endpoint mix.
  4. Test pagination and error handling on the free tier before annual billing.
  5. Confirm whether your project is internal use only or needs redistribution rights.

This is why Polygon.io ranks well in market-data and developer-tool comparisons but should not be positioned as a beginner trading platform. It is infrastructure. When the user needs infrastructure, it is strong. When the user needs a screen with charts and buttons, it is usually the wrong product.

Bottom line

Polygon.io earns its place on a market-data shortlist because the upgrade path is understandable: prototype for free, use delayed data at $29 or $79, and move to $199 when real-time stock data becomes necessary. The main purchasing mistake is choosing a plan by request limit without checking data latency and market entitlements.
