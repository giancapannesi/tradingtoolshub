TraderSync Overview

TraderSync now combines three products in one subscription: a trading journal, the Cypher AI assistant and a historical market-replay simulator. The right tier depends less on basic journaling—which every plan provides—and more on account count, replay precision, Cypher usage and whether the trader needs advanced simulation tools such as Level II, time and sales, screeners or automated backtesting.

This review was checked against TraderSync’s official pricing, feature, trial and billing pages on July 18, 2026. Several figures in the previous TradingToolsHub page were stale, including annual totals, account limits and Elite’s Cypher allowance.

Pro is a journal first, not the complete replay product

Pro supports five active accounts, five strategy checkers and five Cypher messages per day. That is materially more generous than the previous page’s one-account description. It suits a trader who wants to import trades, calculate performance, attach screenshots, track commissions, build checklists and ask a small number of AI questions.

The pricing matrix describes Pro’s market-replay precision as one-minute candles and does not include the full Trade Replay entitlement shown for Premium. TraderSync’s page uses overlapping “Market Replay” and “Trade Replay” labels, so buyers should test the exact workflow they need during the trial. A trader reviewing multi-minute holds may be satisfied; a scalper reconstructing rapid executions will not be.

Premium is the practical tier for active multi-account traders

Premium removes the account and strategy limits, raises Cypher to 15 messages per day and provides one-second replay precision. It is the natural middle tier for prop-firm traders, active futures traders or anyone consolidating several brokers without needing historical Level II.

One-second updates are much more useful than one-minute candles for examining intraday timing, but they are still not a complete reconstruction of every exchange event. Replay is a training approximation based on the historical data TraderSync supplies. It should not be used to claim that a fill would have occurred at a particular price.

Premium does not include Cypher Coach or the advanced Elite market tools. Paying for Premium should be justified by account consolidation and replay detail, not merely by the higher AI message cap.

Elite is a simulator and coaching tier

Elite raises replay resolution to 250 milliseconds and adds Level II, time and sales, watchlists, screeners, key statistics and options-chain tools. It also supports options replay and automated backtesting across supported assets. These features are aimed at scalpers and traders who want to practice market context rather than only review completed trades.

Cypher Coach is exclusive to Elite. Unlike Cypher Assistant, which answers questions, Coach proactively analyzes behavior and delivers feedback. Elite currently allows 60 Cypher messages per day—not unlimited messages as the stale review claimed.

Historical Level II and tape data can improve practice, but they do not reproduce queue position, routing, latency or the trader’s impact on the market. Automated backtests can also overfit. Elite makes sense when these tools are used in a structured practice routine; it is expensive if the user only needs journal reports.

Cypher can find questions faster than it can prove an edge

Cypher uses TraderSync’s internal metrics—including R multiples, expectancy, target quality, price action and timing—to answer questions about imported trades. That can shorten the path from a broad concern to a report worth investigating.

AI feedback is only as reliable as the journal. Missing commissions, duplicated executions, inconsistent setup names or mixed strategies can produce persuasive but invalid conclusions. Before acting on a Cypher observation, open the underlying trades, verify the sample and test the proposed change on later data.

The daily message limits matter because follow-up questions consume the allowance. Pro’s five messages can support a short review; Premium’s 15 allows a deeper conversation; Elite’s 60 is substantial but still finite.

Imports and synchronization need broker-level verification

TraderSync markets connections with more than 700 brokers and platforms. Import support can mean a direct synchronization, a broker export or a formatted upload. “Supported” should not be interpreted as continuous API sync without checking the exact integration.

After the first import, reconcile execution count, position grouping, options multipliers, futures contract values, commissions and timezone against the broker statement. The platform calculates P&L from the data it receives; an omitted fee or malformed partial fill propagates into every report and AI result.

TraderSync supports manual entry for workflows that cannot import cleanly. That preserves coverage but increases maintenance and the risk of selective journaling.

Market Replay now includes real backtesting capability

The previous page said TraderSync had no backtesting or paper-trading capability. That is no longer defensible. TraderSync explicitly markets Market Replay as a way to backtest strategies and practice risk-free with historical data across stocks, futures, crypto and forex, with options support on Elite. Elite also lists automated backtesting.

Replay and automated backtesting solve different tasks. Replay is discretionary practice through a historical session. Automated backtesting applies defined rules across a dataset. Neither proves future profitability, and both need out-of-sample validation.

The simulator page describes 250ms data, multi-chart views, watchlists, screeners, playlists and layouts inspired by familiar trading terminals. Those tools can create deliberate practice, but a trader should define the setup and review criteria before starting or the session becomes chart browsing.

The seven-day trial is the refund protection

TraderSync currently offers a seven-day free trial with access to Elite features. CSV export of trades and executions is excluded during the trial. This is enough time to test a representative import, reports, Cypher and replay if the user prepares broker data beforehand.

Paid subscriptions are non-refundable and TraderSync does not offer pro-rated refunds. Customers may cancel future renewal, but forgetting to cancel does not create refund eligibility. The company also notes that older uninterrupted subscriptions may retain grandfathered rates, while changing or allowing a subscription to lapse can move the customer to current pricing.

Read the billing and refund policy before entering payment details. Use the trial to test the actual broker and workflow rather than exploring generic dashboards.

Mobile access is responsive web access

TraderSync says its responsive interface works across devices, and its NextGen journal specifically describes mobile responsiveness. That supports reviewing trades away from a desktop. Buyers who require offline functionality, native push notifications or a dedicated app-store workflow should test those needs rather than assuming responsive web access provides them.

Complex replay, Level II and multi-chart work will generally remain easier on a larger display even when the interface renders correctly on a phone.

TraderSync versus Edgewonk and Trademetria

TraderSync is strongest when journal analytics and historical replay need to coexist. Edgewonk emphasizes psychology, mistake costing and pattern discovery from a trader’s own history. Trademetria is attractive for broad portfolio accounting, multiple asset classes and cash-flow tracking.

Compare them with the same broker export and the same question. If the task is “show which setup loses after 11 a.m.,” test how quickly each product imports, filters and validates that answer. If the task is deliberate replay practice, TraderSync’s Premium and Elite distinctions become much more important.

Our current assessment

TraderSync is one of the most complete journal-plus-simulator products currently available. Pro is more capable than the old one-account description suggested, Premium is the sensible tier for active multi-account traders, and Elite is a specialized training product rather than a necessary journal upgrade.

The main drawbacks are high annual totals, finite AI allowances, non-refundable payments and the need to verify what each broker connection actually automates. Choose the tier whose account and replay limits solve a real workflow; do not pay for 250ms data and Level II unless they are part of a defined practice process.
