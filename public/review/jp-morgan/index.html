J.P. Morgan Self-Directed Investing Overview

J.P. Morgan Self-Directed Investing is a brokerage account offered directly by JPMorgan Chase, designed to make investing accessible to everyday investors. Launched in 2018 as "You Invest by J.P. Morgan" before rebranding, the platform provides commission-free trading on stocks and ETFs with no account minimum, making it easy for Chase customers to begin investing without barriers.

One of the platform's standout advantages is its deep integration with Chase banking. Customers can instantly transfer funds between their Chase checking, savings, and investment accounts, view all accounts in a single dashboard, and manage their finances holistically through the Chase mobile app or website. This tight ecosystem is a meaningful convenience for the millions of existing Chase customers.

The platform provides access to J.P. Morgan and Morningstar research, basic market data, news feeds, and educational content to help novice investors build their knowledge. Options trading is available at $0.65 per contract. However, the platform lacks advanced tools such as backtesting, paper trading, custom indicators, and futures or forex markets, making it better suited to buy-and-hold investors than active traders.

J.P. Morgan Self-Directed Investing is best for Chase customers seeking a no-frills, low-cost way to invest in stocks, ETFs, mutual funds, and bonds without leaving the Chase ecosystem. Those needing sophisticated charting, automated trading, or broader market access should look at more feature-rich brokers.
