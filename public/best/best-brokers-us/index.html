Best US Brokers in 2026 — Top 10 Compared

Choosing the right US broker affects everything from your commissions to your execution speed to your tax reporting. We tested ten major US-regulated brokers across trading costs, platform quality, account minimums, research tools, and customer support. Fidelity leads for long-term investors with zero-commission stocks and excellent research. Interactive Brokers offers the lowest margin rates and best international market access. Tastytrade was built specifically for options traders with the lowest per-contract fees. TD Ameritrade's thinkorswim platform remains the most powerful free trading platform available. Webull attracts active traders with extended hours and a clean mobile experience. Here is which broker fits your trading style.

The Best US Broker Depends on Product, Order Type, and Account Structure

A long-term ETF investor, active options trader, futures trader, and API developer should not receive the same broker recommendation. Begin with the products you trade, then compare execution tools, fees, margin, cash management, research, platform reliability, and support.

“Commission free” does not mean cost free. Options contracts, futures commissions, regulatory charges, margin interest, market data, routing choices, spreads, and cash-sweep treatment can matter more than the headline stock commission.

Verify Before Transferring an Account

  • Confirm the exact account type and product permissions.
  • Price a normal month of your own trades.
  • Test desktop, web, and mobile workflows with a small balance.
  • Review transfer, withdrawal, inactivity, and data fees.
  • Check official regulatory records and disclosures directly.

Active intraday traders can narrow the field with brokers for day trading.

How We Ranked These Tools

This ranking is built around real trader workflow fit, not just the highest advertised rating or biggest feature list. We compare the tools against the job a trader is actually trying to do, then weigh pricing, ease of use, feature depth, and practical restrictions.

Scoring Criteria

  • • Workflow fit: whether the tool solves a specific trading job better than generic alternatives.
  • • Pricing clarity: published entry price, free tier or trial access, and whether important capabilities are locked behind higher plans.
  • • Feature depth: automation, analytics, broker/data integrations, risk controls, alerts, and reporting where relevant to the category.
  • • Practical usability: how likely a trader is to keep using the tool after the first week, not just whether the feature list looks impressive.
  • • Risk and trust: company maturity, payout or data reliability where relevant, and any restrictions traders need to know before paying.

What To Check Before Choosing

  • • Start with the workflow you need solved, then compare price and features.
  • • Check which features are included in the entry plan rather than assuming the headline price covers everything.
  • • Use the ranking as a shortlist, then verify the current plan details before paying.
