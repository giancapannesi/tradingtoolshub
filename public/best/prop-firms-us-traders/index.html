Best Futures Prop Firms for US Traders (2026)

This page filters the futures prop-firm shortlist for US-based traders without making blanket claims that retail evaluation companies are CFTC-approved. Availability is separate from regulatory status and can change by product, platform, state, and account agreement. Compare Apex Trader Funding, Topstep, Earn2Trade, My Funded Futures, and Tradeify, then confirm current US eligibility, identity requirements, account type, market-data treatment, and payout documentation directly with the provider before purchase.

US Availability Is Not the Same as Regulatory Approval

This page identifies programs that currently provide a route for US-based traders; it does not label retail evaluation companies as CFTC-approved or guarantee that every product is available in every state. Availability, platform access, tax treatment, identity requirements, and account terms can change independently.

Futures-focused programs are the clearest starting point for this audience because they are built around exchange-traded contracts and familiar futures platforms. Before paying, confirm country eligibility on the firm's own checkout or help center and read the agreement attached to the exact evaluation type.

What US Traders Should Verify

  • Whether new US registrations are accepted today.
  • Whether the account is an evaluation, simulated performance account, or live account.
  • Supported exchanges, contracts, platforms, and market-data fees.
  • Identity, business-account, payout, and tax-document requirements.
  • Any state, age, strategy, or professional-data restrictions.

How We Ranked These Tools

This ranking is built around real trader workflow fit, not just the highest advertised rating or biggest feature list. We compare the tools against the job a trader is actually trying to do, then weigh pricing, ease of use, feature depth, and practical restrictions.

Scoring Criteria

  • • Workflow fit: whether the tool solves a specific trading job better than generic alternatives.
  • • Pricing clarity: published entry price, free tier or trial access, and whether important capabilities are locked behind higher plans.
  • • Feature depth: automation, analytics, broker/data integrations, risk controls, alerts, and reporting where relevant to the category.
  • • Practical usability: how likely a trader is to keep using the tool after the first week, not just whether the feature list looks impressive.
  • • Risk and trust: company maturity, payout or data reliability where relevant, and any restrictions traders need to know before paying.

What To Check Before Choosing

  • • Check daily loss, max loss, and trailing drawdown rules before comparing headline account size.
  • • Confirm which platform is actually available for your instrument: futures, forex, CFDs, stocks, or crypto.
  • • Look at payout timing and consistency rules because a cheap challenge can still be a poor fit.
