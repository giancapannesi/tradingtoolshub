Best Free Trading Journals in 2026

Tracking your trades does not require a paid subscription. Several journals offer genuinely useful free tiers that cover basic trade logging and analysis. TraderTrac gives you 30 free trades with AI-powered coaching included — enough to experience the full platform. TradesViz offers unlimited trade imports on their free plan with solid analytics. Tradervue's free tier covers 100 trades per month with basic analysis. Kinfo provides free portfolio tracking with some journaling features. We ranked each free option by import capabilities, analytics quality, and whether the free tier creates artificial limitations that hurt the journaling habit. Starting with a free journal builds the habit first — you can always upgrade once journaling becomes part of your daily trading routine.

A Free Journal Must Be Easy Enough to Use After a Losing Day

The most valuable journal is not the one with the largest dashboard. It is the one that makes trade capture, screenshots, setup tags, mistakes, and weekly review easy enough to become routine. A free plan is useful only if its limits do not break that core loop.

Compare manual entry, broker imports, trade limits, supported asset classes, screenshots, tagging, exports, and whether historical data remains accessible if you stop paying.

Start With a Simple Review Habit

  1. Log every trade for two weeks.
  2. Use a small, consistent set of setup and mistake tags.
  3. Review expectancy, rule adherence, and largest recurring error weekly.
  4. Upgrade only when automation saves meaningful time or reveals a decision you cannot calculate yourself.

How We Ranked These Tools

This ranking is built around real trader workflow fit, not just the highest advertised rating or biggest feature list. We compare the tools against the job a trader is actually trying to do, then weigh pricing, ease of use, feature depth, and practical restrictions.

Scoring Criteria

  • • Workflow fit: whether the tool solves a specific trading job better than generic alternatives.
  • • Pricing clarity: published entry price, free tier or trial access, and whether important capabilities are locked behind higher plans.
  • • Feature depth: automation, analytics, broker/data integrations, risk controls, alerts, and reporting where relevant to the category.
  • • Practical usability: how likely a trader is to keep using the tool after the first week, not just whether the feature list looks impressive.
  • • Risk and trust: company maturity, payout or data reliability where relevant, and any restrictions traders need to know before paying.

What To Check Before Choosing

  • • Prioritize broker import, screenshot capture, tagging speed, and analytics you will actually review every week.
  • • A journal is only valuable if logging is easy enough to become habit.
  • • AI coaching should explain recurring behavior and risk patterns, not just summarize P&L.
